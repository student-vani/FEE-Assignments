Fahrenheit 451, by Ray Bradbury

Fahrenheit 451 is a dystopian novel by Ray Bradbury that presents a future American society where books are outlawed and "firemen" burn any that are found. The novel follows Guy Montag, a fireman who becomes disillusioned with his society’s anti-book stance and joins a resistance group that preserves books by memorizing their contents.

The Hobbit, by J.R.R. Tolkien

The Hobbit is a fantasy novel by J.R.R. Tolkien, which follows the quest of home-loving Bilbo Baggins to win a share of the treasure guarded by Smaug the dragon. Bilbo's journey takes him from his peaceful rural surroundings into more sinister territory, full of dangerous creatures and dark magic.

The Book Thief, by Markus Zusak

The Book Thief is a historical novel by Markus Zusak set during World War II in Germany. It follows the life of Liesel Meminger, a young girl living with her foster parents. Liesel finds solace by stealing books and sharing them with others, while the war and the Nazi regime encroach on her life and the lives of those around her.
